# 03 – Garment Design Standards

## Purpose

This document defines the characteristics of well-designed garments and explains how to evaluate them.

It is a permanent reference manual and does not contain shopping priorities or brand recommendations.

---

# Part I — Garment Fundamentals

This section defines concepts that apply across multiple garment categories.

## 1. Formality Scale

Every garment exists somewhere on a spectrum rather than in a fixed category.

A garment should be evaluated within its intended level of formality rather than against garments serving different purposes.

---

## 2. Fit

Fit describes how a garment follows the body's dimensions.

A good fit should:

* Allow unrestricted movement.
* Follow the body's shape without excessive tightness.
* Avoid unnecessary excess fabric.
* Support the intended silhouette.

Fit is independent of style.

A relaxed-fit garment can fit correctly.

A slim-fit garment can fit poorly.

---

## 3. Silhouette

Silhouette is the visual outline created by the body and clothing together.

Silhouette is more important than numerical size.

The desired silhouette should support the wearer's proportions and intended style identity.

---

## 4. Structure

Structure is the ability of a garment to create or maintain shape.

Sources of structure include:

* Fabric weight
* Interfacing
* Construction
* Collar design
* Shoulder design

Greater structure generally increases visual authority and masculinity, while excessive structure can reduce comfort and appear overly formal.

---

## 5. Drape

Drape describes how fabric hangs on the body.

Good drape should follow the intended silhouette without clinging or collapsing.

---

## 6. Visual Weight

Visual weight is the perceived prominence of a garment.

It is influenced by:

* Colour value
* Texture
* Fabric weight
* Pattern
* Volume

Visual weight should be distributed intentionally to create balanced proportions.

---

## 7. Texture

Texture creates visual interest without relying on loud patterns.

Examples include:

* Oxford cloth
* Twill
* Linen
* Corduroy
* Brushed cotton

Texture is generally preferred over bold prints for long-term versatility.

---

## 8. Construction Quality

Construction determines durability, shape retention and finishing quality.

Indicators include:

* Stitching consistency
* Pattern alignment
* Reinforcement
* Hardware quality
* Seam finishing

Construction should be evaluated independently from branding.

---

## 9. Wardrobe Role

Every garment should perform one primary function within the wardrobe.

Examples:

* Office foundation
* Everyday casual
* Evening smart casual
* Travel
* Seasonal layering

Garments without a clear role should generally be avoided.

---

## 10. Versatility

Versatility is the ability of a garment to integrate naturally into multiple outfits and occasions.

It should be evaluated by practical use rather than theoretical combinations.

# Part II – Shirts

## Philosophy

Shirts define the upper body of an outfit. They frame the face, influence perceived shoulder width, communicate formality and provide the primary visual connection between the wearer and the observer.

For this wardrobe, shirts should prioritize silhouette, versatility and timeless design over trend-driven styling.

---

## Universal Principles

* Prioritize fit before colour.
* Prioritize collar before brand.
* Prioritize fabric before pattern.
* Texture is preferable to loud patterns.
* A shirt should support the outfit rather than dominate it.
* Every shirt should have a clearly defined wardrobe role.
* Choose timeless designs over trend-driven details.

---

## Foundation vs Accent

### Foundation Shirts

Foundation shirts form the core of the wardrobe and should maximise versatility.

Characteristics:

* Timeless colours
* Minimal patterns
* High outfit compatibility
* Suitable for frequent wear

Examples:

* Formal Shirts
* Oxford Cloth Button-Downs
* Casual Button-Downs

### Accent Shirts

Accent shirts introduce seasonal variety, texture or casual character.

Characteristics:

* Seasonal fabrics
* Greater visual texture
* Lower wear frequency
* More specialised use

Examples:

* Linen Shirts
* Flannel Shirts
* Chambray Shirts
* Denim Shirts

# Design Specification

## Fit

### Ideal

* Tailored
* Relaxed Slim
* Clean shoulders
* Gentle waist suppression
* Long enough to remain tucked

### Acceptable

* Slightly relaxed classic fit

### Avoid

* Extra Slim
* Oversized
* Boxy cuts

### Why

Supports a clean silhouette while avoiding shapes that exaggerate a slim frame.

---

## Collar

### Ideal

* Semi-spread
* Moderate spread

### Acceptable

* Classic point

### Avoid

* Extreme cutaway
* Oversized collars
* Button-down collars

### Why

Balances facial proportions, works with rectangular glasses and remains timeless.

---

## Fabric

### Ideal

* Cotton Poplin
* Cotton Twill

### Acceptable

* Pinpoint Oxford (less formal settings)

### Avoid

* Polyester-rich blends
* Linen
* Satin finishes
* High-shine fabrics

### Why

Natural cotton offers better drape, breathability, longevity and refinement.

---

## Construction

### Ideal

* Medium collar structure
* Quality stitching
* Single barrel cuffs
* Clean placket
* Minimal branding

### Acceptable

* French cuffs (occasion dependent)

### Avoid

* Soft collapsing collars
* Decorative trims
* Excessive branding

### Why

Improves durability and keeps the shirt visually refined.

---

## Colours

### Ideal

* White
* Off-white
* Light Blue

### Acceptable

* Pale Cream

### Avoid

* Loud colours
* Busy patterns

### Why

Provides maximum versatility while complementing a Deep Autumn palette.

---

## Pattern

### Ideal

* Solid
* Fine Stripe

### Acceptable

* Small conservative check

### Avoid

* Large checks
* Bold stripes
* High-contrast prints

### Why

Allows the tailoring—not the shirt—to remain the visual focus.

---

## Outfit Compatibility

### Excellent

* Suits
* Blazers
* Dress trousers
* Derbies
* Oxfords

### Good

* Chinos

### Poor

* Jeans
* Shorts
* Casual sneakers

---

## Personal Notes

* Prioritize fit and collar quality over brand.
* Relaxed Slim is preferred over Extra Slim.
* Warm whites are preferable where dress code allows.

---

## Avoid

* Poor shoulder fit
* Transparent fabric
* Polyester-rich composition
* Collar collapse
* Loud branding
* Fashion-forward detailing

* Straight body
* Full sleeve length (rollable)

#### Acceptable

* Tailored

#### Avoid

* Extra Slim
* Oversized

#### Acceptable

#### Avoid

* Polyester-rich blends
* Thin transparent linen
* High-stretch fabrics

#### Why

* Maximum breathability.
* Beautiful natural texture.
* Develops character with wear.
* Linen wrinkles are part of its appeal, not a defect.

---

### Construction

#### Ideal

* Matte buttons
* Minimal branding
* Rounded hem

#### Acceptable

* Single chest pocket

#### Avoid

* Contrast trims

* Ecru

* Sage
* Sand
* Tobacco

#### Acceptable

* Rust
* Terracotta
* Muted Beige
* Warm White

#### Avoid

* Bright Blue
* Neon
* Cool Grey
* Black (for most warm-weather use)

#### Why

* Earthy colours complement linen's natural texture.
* Harmonises with a Deep Autumn palette.
* Maintains a relaxed, sophisticated appearance.

---

### Pattern

#### Acceptable

#### Avoid

* Large checks
* Loud florals
* High-contrast prints

#### Why

# Part III — Trousers

## Trouser Philosophy

Trousers are the visual foundation of an outfit. They determine overall proportions, influence perceived height, and contribute significantly to silhouette.

Unlike shirts, poor trouser fit is difficult to disguise.

## Universal Principles

- Prioritize rise before colour.
- Prioritize fit before fabric.
- Prioritize silhouette before trend.
- Never compromise on rise or fit for a more desirable colour.
- Choose timeless cuts over trend-driven silhouettes.
- Trousers should create balance rather than draw attention.

These principles apply to every trouser category in this document.

## Foundation vs Accent

### Foundation Trousers

Foundation trousers should maximize versatility and outfit compatibility.

Characteristics:

Neutral colours
Timeless silhouettes
Minimal detailing
High outfit repeatability

Examples:

Dress Trousers
Chinos
Dark Indigo Jeans

### Accent Trousers

Accent trousers introduce texture, seasonality or visual interest.

They should complement—not replace—the foundation.

Characteristics:

More distinctive fabrics
Seasonal materials
Greater visual texture
Lower wardrobe frequency

Examples:

Linen Trousers
Corduroy Trousers

# Design Specification

## Rise

### Ideal

* Mid Rise
* Slightly High Rise

### Acceptable

* True Mid Rise

### Avoid

* Low Rise

### Why

* Improves leg proportions.
* Creates a cleaner silhouette.
* Supports tucked shirts.
* Appears more masculine and timeless.

---

## Fit

### Ideal

* Tailored
* Relaxed Slim
* Clean seat
* Comfortable thigh

### Acceptable

* Straight Fit

### Avoid

* Skinny
* Baggy
* Oversized

### Why

* Creates clean lines without restricting movement.
* Flatters a slim build without exaggerating thinness.

---

## Leg Shape

### Ideal

* Straight
* Gentle taper below the knee

### Acceptable

* Classic straight

### Avoid

* Aggressive taper
* Wide leg
* Balloon fit

### Why

* Balances the upper and lower body.
* Remains timeless across changing trends.

---

## Hem & Break

### Ideal

* No Break
* Slight Break

### Acceptable

* Quarter Break

### Avoid

* Heavy stacking
* Full Break
* Cropped lengths

### Why

* Produces a cleaner, more refined silhouette.
* Keeps attention on the overall outfit rather than excess fabric.

---

## Fabric

### Ideal

* 100% Wool
* Tropical Wool
* Wool Blend (high wool content)

### Acceptable

* Wool-Poly Blend (quality focused)

### Avoid

* Polyester-dominant fabrics
* Shiny fabrics
* Thin synthetic materials

### Why

* Superior drape.
* Better longevity.
* Elegant movement.
* Suitable across seasons.

---

## Construction

### Ideal

* Flat Front
* Clean waistband
* Minimal branding
* Quality stitching
* Side adjusters (optional)

### Acceptable

* Single pleat (if well executed)

### Avoid

* Cargo pockets
* Decorative hardware
* Excessive branding

### Why

* Simplicity improves versatility.
* Clean construction keeps the focus on silhouette.

---

## Colours

### Ideal

* Charcoal
* Dark Grey
* Warm Navy

### Acceptable

* Mid Grey
* Chocolate Brown

### Avoid

* Bright colours
* Cool light grey
* Loud patterns

### Why

* Easy integration with tailoring.
* Timeless and versatile.
* Complements a Deep Autumn palette.

---

## Pattern

### Ideal

* Solid
* Fine texture

### Acceptable

* Subtle pinstripe
* Birdseye

### Avoid

* Bold checks
* High-contrast patterns

### Why

* Keeps the trousers versatile.
* Allows jackets and shirts to provide visual interest.

---

## Outfit Compatibility

### Excellent

* Formal Shirts
* Blazers
* Suits
* Derby Shoes
* Oxford Shoes

### Good

* Fine Knitwear
* OCBDs (business casual)

### Poor

* Graphic T-shirts
* Hoodies
* Athletic Sneakers

---

## Personal Notes

* Prioritize rise and drape over brand.
* A clean silhouette is more valuable than trend-driven details.
* Mid to slightly high rise consistently improves my proportions.
* Avoid extra-slim tailoring.

---

## Avoid

* Low rise
* Skinny fit
* Heavy break
* Polyester-dominant fabrics
* Decorative details
* Loud branding

---
