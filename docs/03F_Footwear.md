# 03 – Garment Specifications

## Purpose

This document defines the characteristics of well-designed garments and explains how to evaluate them.

It is a permanent reference manual and does not contain shopping priorities or brand recommendations.

---

# Part I — Garment Fundamentals

This section defines concepts that apply across all garment categories.

## 1. Formality Scale

Every garment exists somewhere on a spectrum rather than in a fixed category.

- Formal
- Business
- Business Casual
- Smart Casual
- Casual
- Relaxed Casual

A garment should be evaluated within its intended level of formality rather than against garments serving different purposes.

---

## 2. Fit

Fit describes how a garment follows the body's dimensions.

A good fit should:

- Allow unrestricted movement
- Follow the body's shape without excessive tightness
- Avoid unnecessary excess fabric
- Support the intended silhouette

Fit is independent of style.

A relaxed-fit garment can fit correctly.

A slim-fit garment can fit poorly.

---

## 3. Silhouette

Silhouette is the visual outline created by the body and clothing together.

Silhouette is more important than numerical size.

The desired silhouette should support the wearer's proportions and intended style identity.

---

## 4. Structure

Structure is the ability of a garment to create or maintain shape.

Sources of structure include:

- Fabric weight
- Interfacing
- Construction
- Collar design
- Shoulder design

Greater structure generally increases visual authority and masculinity, while excessive structure can reduce comfort and appear overly formal.

---

## 5. Drape

Drape describes how fabric hangs on the body.

Good drape should follow the intended silhouette without clinging or collapsing.

---

## 6. Visual Weight

Visual weight is the perceived prominence of a garment.

It is influenced by:

- Colour value
- Texture
- Fabric weight
- Pattern
- Volume

Visual weight should be distributed intentionally to create balanced proportions.

---

## 7. Texture

Texture creates visual interest without relying on loud patterns.

Examples include:

- Oxford cloth
- Twill
- Linen
- Corduroy
- Brushed cotton

Texture is generally preferred over bold prints for long-term versatility.

---

## 8. Construction Quality

Construction determines durability, shape retention and finishing quality.

Indicators include:

- Stitching consistency
- Pattern alignment
- Reinforcement
- Hardware quality
- Seam finishing

Construction should be evaluated independently from branding.

---

## 9. Wardrobe Role

Every garment should perform one primary function within the wardrobe.

Examples:

- Office foundation
- Everyday casual
- Evening smart casual
- Travel
- Seasonal layering

Garments without a clear role should generally be avoided.

---

## 10. Versatility

Versatility is the ability of a garment to integrate naturally into multiple outfits and occasions.

It should be evaluated by practical use rather than theoretical combinations.

---

# Part VII – Footwear

## Philosophy

Footwear is the foundation of every outfit and has a disproportionate influence on perceived formality, refinement and visual balance.

Unlike clothing, shoes should be evaluated primarily by their shape, construction and materials rather than branding or trend.

For this wardrobe, footwear should prioritize timeless design, versatility and longevity.

---

## Universal Principles

* Prioritize shape before colour.
* Prioritize construction before brand.
* Prioritize leather quality before finish.
* Buy fewer, higher-quality pairs.
* Avoid trend-driven silhouettes.
* Shoes should complement the outfit rather than dominate it.

---

## Foundation vs Accent

### Foundation Footwear

Shoes worn frequently across multiple occasions.

Characteristics:

* Maximum versatility
* Timeless styling
* Neutral colours
* High outfit compatibility

Examples:

* Minimal Leather Sneakers
* Penny Loafers
* Derby Shoes

### Accent Footwear

Shoes intended for specific seasons or occasions.

Characteristics:

* Lower wear frequency
* More specialized styling

Examples:

* Chelsea Boots
* Chukka Boots
* Oxford Shoes
* Sandals

---

## Common Pitfalls

These issues appear across multiple footwear types:

* **Trend-driven silhouettes** — Dated shoe shapes look outdated quickly; timeless lasts endure
* **Poor construction** — Weak stitching, thin soles, or inferior welting compromises longevity
* **Synthetic or faux leather** — Doesn't age well or develop patina; cracks and peels
* **Excessive detailing or branding** — Should complement the outfit, not draw attention
* **Incompatible toe shapes or proportions** — Extreme pointed or oversized toes conflict with modern minimalist aesthetics

## Categories

## 7.1 Minimal Leather Sneakers

### Importance Tier
Tier S — Foundation

### Quick Spec

| Category     | Ideal                                   |
| ------------ | --------------------------------------- |
| Last & Shape | Low Profile, Rounded or Soft Almond Toe |
| Upper        | Full-Grain Leather                      |
| Construction | Cup Sole                                |
| Sole         | Low-Profile Rubber                      |
| Colours      | Off-White, Cream, White                 |
| Details      | Minimal Branding                        |

---

### Role

The foundation casual shoe of the wardrobe.

Provides the greatest versatility across casual and smart-casual outfits while maintaining a refined appearance.

---

### When I Need It

* Smart casual office
* Casual Friday
* Weekends
* Date nights
* Travel
* Everyday wear

---

### Design Specification

#### Last & Shape

##### Ideal

* Low-profile silhouette
* Rounded or soft almond toe
* Clean proportions
* Moderate toe box
* Minimal visual bulk

##### Acceptable

* Slightly elongated toe

##### Avoid

* Bulky athletic silhouettes
* Square toes
* Aggressively pointed toes
* Oversized platform soles

##### Why

The last determines how refined a sneaker appears and has the greatest influence on versatility.

---

#### Upper Material

##### Ideal

* Full-Grain Leather
* Smooth matte finish

##### Acceptable

* Top-Grain Leather

##### Avoid

* Faux leather
* Corrected leather
* Patent leather
* Heavy synthetic panels

##### Why

Quality leather develops character, ages gracefully and complements a refined wardrobe.

---

#### Construction

##### Ideal

* Cup sole
* Clean stitching
* Minimal panel construction
* Quality lining

##### Acceptable

* Well-executed cemented construction

##### Avoid

* Excessive overlays
* Visible air units
* Complex technical designs

##### Why

Simple construction keeps the sneaker timeless and easy to pair with a wide range of outfits.

---

#### Sole

##### Ideal

* Low-profile rubber sole
* White or off-white sidewall
* Minimal tread

##### Acceptable

* Warm gum sole

##### Avoid

* Thick platform soles
* Aggressive lug soles
* Contrast-coloured midsoles

##### Why

The sole should support the silhouette rather than dominate it.

---

#### Colours

##### Ideal

* Off-White
* Cream

##### Acceptable

* White
* Taupe

##### Avoid

* Black
* Bright colours
* Neon
* Colour blocking

##### Why

Off-white and cream integrate more naturally with a Deep Autumn wardrobe while remaining highly versatile.

---

#### Details

##### Ideal

* Minimal branding
* Tonal stitching
* Plain laces
* Clean heel

##### Acceptable

* Small embossed logo

##### Avoid

##### Why

The sneaker should act as a foundation rather than a statement piece.

---

### Outfit Compatibility

#### Excellent

* Chinos
* Dark Indigo Jeans
* Linen Trousers
* Shorts
* OCBDs
* Casual Button-Downs
* Polo Shirts
* Knit Polos
* Overshirts
* Harrington Jackets

#### Good

* Crewneck Sweaters
* Chore Jackets

#### Poor

* Business Suits
* Tuxedos
* Black Tie

---

### Wardrobe Notes

* One of the highest-ROI purchases in the wardrobe.
* Prioritize shape before brand.
* Off-white and cream should generally be preferred over optical white.
* Keep the design as clean as possible.

---

### Avoid

* Running shoe silhouettes
* Chunky "dad" sneakers
* Oversized soles
* Faux leather
* Large logos
* Heavy colour blocking
* Trend-driven designs

---

---

### When I Need It

* Smart casual office
* Date nights
* Dinner
* Weddings
* Travel

---

### Design Specification

#### Last & Shape

##### Ideal

* Rounded or soft almond toe
* Moderate forefoot volume
* Clean proportions
* Low-profile silhouette

##### Acceptable

* Slightly elongated toe

##### Avoid

* Narrow Italian lasts
* Square toes
* Aggressively pointed toes
* Chunky fashion loafers

##### Why

The last determines both elegance and long-term versatility. Balanced proportions age better than fashion-driven shapes.

---

#### Vamp & Apron

##### Ideal

* Medium vamp
* Clean hand-sewn apron
* Balanced penny strap
* Moderate opening

##### Acceptable

* Slightly higher vamp

##### Avoid

* Very high vamp
* Bulky apron stitching
* Decorative tassels
* Horsebit hardware

##### Why

Balanced vamp proportions make the loafer suitable across both casual and smart-casual outfits without becoming overly formal or overly casual.

---

#### Upper Material

##### Ideal

* Full-Grain Leather
* Suede

##### Acceptable

* Nubuck

##### Avoid

* Faux leather
* Patent leather
* Corrected leather

##### Why

Quality materials improve ageing, comfort and overall refinement.

---

#### Construction

##### Ideal

* Blake Stitch
* Goodyear Welt

##### Acceptable

* High-quality cemented construction

##### Avoid

* Heavy synthetic construction
* Visible glue
* Excessive decorative stitching

##### Why

Good construction improves longevity while maintaining a sleek profile.

---

#### Sole

##### Ideal

* Leather Sole
* Thin Rubber Sole

##### Acceptable

* Combination Sole

##### Avoid

* Thick lug soles
* Chunky platform soles

##### Why

A slim sole preserves the loafer's elegant proportions.

---

#### Colours

##### Ideal

* Dark Brown
* Chocolate
* Tobacco

##### Acceptable

* Dark Olive Suede
* Espresso

##### Avoid

* Black (first pair)
* Bright colours
* Two-tone finishes

##### Why

Brown tones integrate naturally with a Deep Autumn wardrobe and offer greater versatility than black.

---

#### Details

##### Ideal

* Penny strap
* Minimal branding
* Tonal stitching

##### Acceptable

* Hand-sewn apron

##### Avoid

##### Why

Simple details preserve timelessness and maximize outfit compatibility.

---

### Outfit Compatibility

* Linen Trousers
* OCBDs
* Polo Shirts
* Knit Polos
* Blazers
