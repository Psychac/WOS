# 03 – Garment Specifications

## Purpose

This document defines the characteristics of well-designed garments and explains how to evaluate them.

It is a permanent reference manual and does not contain shopping priorities or brand recommendations.

---

# Part I — Garment Fundamentals

This section defines concepts that apply across multiple garment categories.

## 1. Formality Scale

Every garment exists somewhere on a spectrum rather than in a fixed category.

- Formal
- Business
- Business Casual
- Smart Casual
- Casual
- Relaxed Casual

A garment should be evaluated within its intended level of formality rather than against garments serving different purposes.

---

## 2. Fit

Fit describes how a garment follows the body's dimensions.

A A good fit should:

- Allow unrestricted movement
- Follow the body's shape without excessive tightness
- Avoid unnecessary excess fabric
- Support the intended silhouette

Fit is independent of style.

A relaxed-fit garment can fit correctly.

A slim-fit garment can fit poorly.

---

## 3. Silhouette

Silhouette is the visual outline created by the body and clothing together.

Silhouette is more important than numerical size.

The desired silhouette should support the wearer's proportions and intended style identity.

---

## 4. Structure

Structure is the ability of a garment to create or maintain shape.

Sources of structure include:

- Fabric weight
- Interfacing
- Construction
- Collar design
- Shoulder design

Greater structure generally increases visual authority and masculinity, while excessive structure can reduce comfort and appear overly formal.

---

## 5. Drape

Drape describes how fabric hangs on the body.

Good drape should follow the intended silhouette without clinging or collapsing.

---

## 6. Visual Weight

Visual weight is the perceived prominence of a garment.

It is influenced by:

- Colour value
- Texture
- Fabric weight
- Pattern
- Volume

Visual weight should be distributed intentionally to create balanced proportions.

---

## 7. Texture

Texture creates visual interest without relying on loud patterns.

Examples include:

- Oxford cloth
- Twill
- Linen
- Corduroy
- Brushed cotton

Texture is generally preferred over bold prints for long-term versatility.

---

## 8. Construction Quality

Construction determines durability, shape retention and finishing quality.

Indicators include:

- Stitching consistency
- Pattern alignment
- Reinforcement
- Hardware quality
- Seam finishing

Construction should be evaluated independently from branding.

---

## 9. Wardrobe Role

Every garment should perform one primary function within the wardrobe.

Examples:

- Office foundation
- Everyday casual
- Evening smart casual
- Travel
- Seasonal layering

Garments without a clear role should generally be avoided.

---

## 10. Versatility

Versatility is the ability of a garment to integrate naturally into multiple outfits and occasions.

It should be evaluated by practical use rather than theoretical combinations.

# Part VI – Tailoring

## Philosophy

Tailoring is the most structured category in the wardrobe and represents the highest level of refinement.

Unlike casual clothing, tailoring should enhance the natural proportions of the body through precise fit, clean lines, and thoughtful construction.

For this wardrobe, tailoring should prioritize proportion, structure, and timeless elegance over fashion trends.

---

## Universal Principles

* Prioritize shoulder fit above all else.
* Prioritize silhouette before colour.
* Structure should enhance—not exaggerate—the physique.
* Natural fabrics are strongly preferred.
* Buy fewer, higher-quality tailoring pieces.
* Classic proportions age better than fashion-forward cuts.

---

## Foundation vs Accent

### Foundation Tailoring

The core tailoring pieces suitable for most formal and business occasions.

Examples:

* Navy Suit
* Blazer

### Accent Tailoring

Specialized garments for specific occasions or personal expression.

Examples:

* Sport Coat
* Waistcoat
* Tuxedo

---

## Common Pitfalls

These issues appear across multiple tailoring types:

* **Poor shoulder fit** — The foundation of all tailoring; cannot be corrected elsewhere
* **Fashion-forward or oversized cuts** — Classic proportions outlast trends and suit various body types better
* **Heavy branding or decorative buttons** — Detracts from clean lines and timeless elegance
* **Synthetic or low-quality fabrics** — Natural fabrics drape better and age with character
* **Poor construction** — Half-canvas or better is essential for longevity and shape retention

---

## Categories

## 6.1 Blazer

### Importance Tier
Tier A — Core

### Quick Spec

| Category     | Ideal                         |
| ------------ | ----------------------------- |
| Silhouette   | Structured, Single-Breasted   |
| Fit          | Tailored                      |
| Shoulders    | Lightly Structured, Natural   |
| Fabric       | Wool or Wool Blend            |
| Construction | Half-Canvas or Quality Fused  |
| Colours      | Warm Navy, Chocolate, Tobacco |
| Pattern      | Solid or Subtle Texture       |

---

### Role

The foundation tailored garment of the wardrobe.

Bridges formal and smart-casual dressing while providing structure, refinement and versatility.

---

### When I Need It

* Smart casual office
* Business meetings
* Date nights
* Weddings
* Dinner
* Semi-formal events
* Travel

---

### Design Specification

#### Silhouette

##### Ideal

* Single-breasted
* Two-button closure
* Moderate lapels
* Jacket covering the seat
* Balanced button stance

##### Acceptable

* Three-roll-two front

##### Avoid

* Very short jackets
* Long fashion cuts
* Double-breasted (first blazer)

##### Why

* A classic silhouette maximizes versatility.
* Proper length improves body proportions.
* Two-button designs transition effortlessly between smart-casual and formal settings.

---

#### Shoulders

##### Ideal

* Lightly structured
* Natural shoulder line
* Clean sleeve head

##### Acceptable

* Soft Neapolitan shoulder

##### Avoid

* Heavy padding
* Dropped shoulders
* Rope shoulders

##### Why

* Light structure broadens the upper body subtly.
* Enhances a slim frame without appearing rigid.

---

#### Fabric

##### Ideal

* 100% Wool
* Tropical Wool
* Fresco Wool

##### Acceptable

* Wool Blend (high wool content)

##### Avoid

* Polyester-dominant blends
* High-shine fabrics
* Heavy synthetic textures

##### Why

* Wool offers the best drape, breathability and longevity.
* Matte fabrics complement a refined, understated wardrobe.

---

#### Fit

##### Ideal

* Tailored
* Clean chest
* Suppressed waist
* Comfortable sleeve mobility

##### Acceptable

* Relaxed tailoring

##### Avoid

* Skinny fit
* Boxy fit
* Oversized silhouettes

##### Why

* A tailored fit creates a masculine V-shape while remaining comfortable for extended wear.

---

#### Construction

##### Ideal

* Half-canvas
* Double vents
* Functional inner pockets
* Quality horn-style buttons
* Minimal branding

##### Acceptable

* Well-made fused construction

##### Avoid

* Single vent

##### Why

* Better construction improves drape, durability and ease of movement.

---

#### Colours

##### Ideal

* Warm Navy
* Chocolate Brown
* Tobacco

##### Acceptable

* Charcoal Brown
* Olive (casual blazer)

##### Avoid

* Bright Blue
* Jet Black
* Loud colours

##### Why

* Warm neutrals integrate naturally with the rest of the wardrobe.
* Navy remains the most versatile tailored colour.

---

#### Pattern

##### Ideal

* Solid
* Birdseye
* Hopsack
* Fine texture

##### Acceptable

* Prince of Wales (low contrast)

##### Avoid

* Bold windowpane
* Loud checks
* High-contrast patterns

##### Why

* Subtle texture provides interest while preserving versatility.

---

### Outfit Compatibility

#### Excellent

* OCBDs
* Formal Shirts
* Polo Shirts
* Knit Polos
* Crewneck Sweaters
* Chinos
* Dress Trousers
* Dark Indigo Jeans
* Loafers
* Derby Shoes

#### Good

* Linen Trousers
* Chelsea Boots

#### Poor

* Shorts
* Graphic T-Shirts
* Athletic Sneakers

---

### Wardrobe Notes

* One of the highest-ROI tailored garments in the wardrobe.
* Prioritize silhouette and shoulders over brand.
* Warm navy should generally be the first blazer colour.
* A quality blazer can replace a suit jacket in many smart-casual situations.

---

### Avoid

* Skinny tailoring
* Excessive shoulder padding
* Short cropped jackets
* Polyester-dominant fabrics
* Loud patterns
* Large logos

---

## 6.4 Waistcoat

### Importance Tier
Tier C — Specialist

### Quick Spec

| Category     | Ideal                        |
| ------------ | ---------------------------- |
| Silhouette   | Tailored                     |
| Fit          | Close but Comfortable        |
| Fabric       | Matching Suit Fabric or Wool |
| Construction | Single-Breasted              |
| Colours      | Match Suit                   |
| Pattern      | Solid                        |

---

### Role

An optional tailoring piece providing additional refinement and warmth.

Primarily intended as part of a three-piece suit rather than a standalone garment.

---

### When I Need It

* Weddings
* Formal ceremonies
* Winter tailoring
* Business formality (occasion dependent)

---

### Design Specification

#### Silhouette

##### Ideal

* Five or six-button single-breasted
* Covers waistband completely
* Clean V-opening

##### Avoid

* Fashion waistcoats
* Very short lengths

#### Fit

##### Ideal

* Close to the body
* Comfortable movement

##### Avoid

* Tight pulling
* Excess fabric

#### Fabric

##### Ideal

* Matching suit cloth
* Wool

##### Avoid

* Shiny satin (except formal eveningwear)

#### Colours

##### Ideal

* Match the suit

##### Acceptable

* Tonal neutral (for separates)

##### Avoid

* Contrasting novelty colours

##### Why

The waistcoat should integrate seamlessly into the tailoring rather than becoming the focal point.

---

### Outfit Compatibility

#### Excellent

* Matching Suit
* Formal Shirt
* Tie

#### Poor

* Jeans
* Casual Trousers
* T-Shirts

---

### Wardrobe Notes

* Buy only after the core tailoring wardrobe is complete.
* Treat as an enhancement rather than a necessity.

---

### Avoid

* Novelty backs
* Loud patterns
* Skinny fits
* Decorative chains
