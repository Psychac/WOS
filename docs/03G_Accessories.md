# 03 – Garment Specifications

## Purpose

This document defines the characteristics of well-designed garments and explains how to evaluate them.

It is a permanent reference manual and does not contain shopping priorities or brand recommendations.

---

# Part I — Garment Fundamentals

This section defines concepts that apply across multiple garment categories.

## 1. Formality Scale

Every garment exists somewhere on a spectrum rather than in a fixed category.

- Formal
- Business
- Business Casual
- Smart Casual
- Casual
- Relaxed Casual

A garment should be evaluated within its intended level of formality rather than against garments serving different purposes.

---

## 2. Fit

Fit describes how a garment follows the body's dimensions.

A A good fit should:

- Allow unrestricted movement
- Follow the body's shape without excessive tightness
- Avoid unnecessary excess fabric
- Support the intended silhouette

Fit is independent of style.

A relaxed-fit garment can fit correctly.

A slim-fit garment can fit poorly.

---

## 3. Silhouette

Silhouette is the visual outline created by the body and clothing together.

Silhouette is more important than numerical size.

The desired silhouette should support the wearer's proportions and intended style identity.

---

## 4. Structure

Structure is the ability of a garment to create or maintain shape.

Sources of structure include:

- Fabric weight
- Interfacing
- Construction
- Collar design
- Shoulder design

Greater structure generally increases visual authority and masculinity, while excessive structure can reduce comfort and appear overly formal.

---

## 5. Drape

Drape describes how fabric hangs on the body.

Good drape should follow the intended silhouette without clinging or collapsing.

---

## 6. Visual Weight

Visual weight is the perceived prominence of a garment.

It is influenced by:

- Colour value
- Texture
- Fabric weight
- Pattern
- Volume

Visual weight should be distributed intentionally to create balanced proportions.

---

## 7. Texture

Texture creates visual interest without relying on loud patterns.

Examples include:

- Oxford cloth
- Twill
- Linen
- Corduroy
- Brushed cotton

Texture is generally preferred over bold prints for long-term versatility.

---

## 8. Construction Quality

Construction determines durability, shape retention and finishing quality.

Indicators include:

- Stitching consistency
- Pattern alignment
- Reinforcement
- Hardware quality
- Seam finishing

Construction should be evaluated independently from branding.

---

## 9. Wardrobe Role

Every garment should perform one primary function within the wardrobe.

Examples:

- Office foundation
- Everyday casual
- Evening smart casual
- Travel
- Seasonal layering

Garments without a clear role should generally be avoided.

---

## 10. Versatility

Versatility is the ability of a garment to integrate naturally into multiple outfits and occasions.

It should be evaluated by practical use rather than theoretical combinations.

# Part VIII – Accessories

## Philosophy

Accessories complete an outfit through subtle refinement rather than visual prominence.

For this wardrobe, accessories should reinforce cohesion, functionality and timelessness while avoiding unnecessary attention.

---

## Universal Principles

* Accessories should complement rather than dominate.
* Prioritize quality over quantity.
* Leather should coordinate across the outfit where practical.
* Metal finishes should remain consistent within an outfit.
* Avoid trend-driven statement accessories.
* Every accessory should have a functional purpose.

---

## Foundation vs Accent

### Foundation Accessories

Frequently used essentials that support nearly every outfit.

Examples:

* Leather Belt
* Watch
* Sunglasses

### Accent Accessories

Occasional pieces that introduce seasonal function or subtle personality.

Examples:

* Scarf
* Pocket Square
* Gloves
* Cap

---

## Common Pitfalls

These issues appear across multiple accessory types:

* **Heavy branding or logos** — Detracts from the principle that accessories should complement, not dominate
* **Trend-driven designs** — Statement accessories date quickly; timeless pieces remain relevant
* **Synthetic or low-quality materials** — Reduces longevity and professional appearance
* **Incompatible metal finishes** — Creates visual discord when mixed with existing accessories
* **Excessive detailing** — Conflicts with the goal of subtle refinement

---

## Categories

## 8.1 Leather Belt

### Importance Tier
Tier S — Foundation

### Quick Spec

| Category     | Ideal                                       |
| ------------ | ------------------------------------------- |
| Width        | 3–3.5 cm                                    |
| Leather      | Full-Grain Leather                          |
| Construction | Single-Piece Leather with Clean Edge Finish |
| Buckle       | Simple Brushed Silver                       |
| Colours      | Dark Brown, Chocolate, Tobacco              |
| Details      | Minimal                                     |

---

### Role

The foundation accessory of the wardrobe.

Provides visual continuity between footwear and clothing while maintaining a refined appearance.

---

### When I Need It

* Smart casual office
* Business meetings
* Date nights
* Weddings
* Everyday wear with chinos or dress trousers

---

### Design Specification

#### Width

##### Ideal

* 3–3.5 cm

##### Acceptable

* 2.8–3.8 cm

##### Avoid

* Very narrow fashion belts
* Wide casual belts

##### Why

A medium-width belt balances versatility, working equally well with chinos, dress trousers and dark denim.

---

#### Leather

##### Ideal

* Full-Grain Leather

##### Acceptable

* Top-Grain Leather

##### Avoid

* Bonded leather
* Faux leather
* Split leather

##### Why

Full-grain leather develops character over time, resists premature cracking and better matches the quality standards of the wardrobe.

---

#### Construction

##### Ideal

* Single-piece leather
* Clean painted or burnished edges
* Straight stitching
* Minimal visible reinforcement

##### Acceptable

* Quality stitched construction

##### Avoid

* Laminated construction
* Thick padded belts
* Decorative embossing

##### Why

Simple construction improves longevity and keeps the belt visually understated.

---

#### Buckle

##### Ideal

* Brushed silver
* Rectangular or softly rounded frame
* Single-prong buckle

##### Acceptable

* Matte gunmetal

##### Avoid

* Oversized buckles
* Polished gold
* Decorative logos
* Western buckles

##### Why

A restrained buckle complements the outfit rather than drawing attention to itself.

---

#### Colours

##### Ideal

* Dark Brown
* Chocolate
* Tobacco

##### Acceptable

* Espresso

##### Avoid

* Black (first belt)

##### Why

Brown belts integrate naturally with the wardrobe's earth-toned footwear and Deep Autumn palette.

---

#### Details

##### Ideal

* Minimal branding
* Tonal stitching
* Clean keeper loops

##### Acceptable

* Small embossed maker's mark

##### Avoid
* Reversible construction

##### Why

Small details preserve versatility and prevent the belt from becoming a focal point.

---

### Outfit Compatibility

#### Excellent

* Chinos
* Dress Trousers
* Dark Indigo Jeans
* Loafers
* Derby Shoes
* Chelsea Boots

#### Good

* Linen Trousers

#### Poor

* Shorts
* Athletic Wear

---

### Wardrobe Notes

* One high-quality dark brown belt will satisfy the majority of wardrobe needs.
* Prioritize leather quality over designer branding.
* Match the belt to the footwear family, and replace it only when structure begins to fail.

---

### Avoid

* Bonded leather
* Oversized buckles
* Reversible belts
* Heavy contrast stitching
* Fashion logos
* Artificial distressing

---

## 8.2 Watch

### Importance Tier
Tier S — Foundation

### Quick Spec

| Category         | Ideal                                   |
| ---------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| Case Proportions | 36–38 mm, Thin Profile                  |
| Dial             | Clean, Minimal                          |
| Strap            | Brown Leather or Brushed Steel Bracelet |
| Movement         | Quartz or Automatic                     |
| Colours          | Silver Case, White/Cream/Black Dial     |
| Details          | Minimal                                 |

---

### Role

The foundation accessory of the wardrobe.

Provides refinement and visual balance while complementing both casual and tailored outfits.

---

### When I Need It

* Everyday wear
* Smart casual office
* Business meetings
* Date nights
* Weddings
* Travel

---

### Design Specification

#### Case Proportions

##### Ideal

* 36–38 mm diameter
* Thin case
* Balanced lug length
* Medium bezel

##### Acceptable

* 39 mm

##### Avoid

* Oversized cases (40 mm+ for this wardrobe)
* Thick dive-watch proportions
* Extremely small fashion watches

##### Why

Balanced proportions suit a slimmer wrist and maintain elegance across casual and formal outfits.

---

#### Dial

##### Ideal

* Clean dial
* Applied indices
* Simple date window (optional)
* Minimal text

##### Acceptable

* Small seconds

##### Avoid

* Skeleton dials
* Oversized numerals
* Busy chronographs
* Bright accent colours

##### Why

A restrained dial remains timeless and integrates naturally with every outfit.

---

#### Strap / Bracelet

##### Ideal

* Dark Brown Leather Strap
* Brushed Stainless Steel Bracelet

##### Acceptable

* Chocolate leather
* Tan leather (casual)

##### Avoid

* Bright coloured straps
* Silicone (outside sport)
* Large deployant hardware

##### Why

Leather and brushed steel provide the greatest versatility.

---

#### Movement

##### Ideal

* Reliable Quartz
* Automatic Mechanical

##### Acceptable

* Solar Quartz

##### Avoid

* Novelty movements
* Fashion-brand movements

##### Why

Reliability and longevity are more valuable than unnecessary complexity.

---

#### Colours

##### Ideal

* Silver case
* White dial
* Cream dial
* Black dial

##### Acceptable

* Champagne dial

##### Avoid

##### Why

Neutral colours integrate effortlessly with the wardrobe.

---

#### Details

##### Ideal

* Minimal branding
* Sapphire crystal (preferred)
* Brushed finishing

##### Acceptable

* Mineral crystal

##### Avoid

* Decorative bezels
* Excessive polishing
* Large logos

##### Why

The watch should communicate refinement rather than attract attention.

---

### Outfit Compatibility

#### Excellent

* Every tailoring category
* All shirts
* All knitwear
* Chinos
* Dress Trousers
* Dark Indigo Jeans

#### Good

* Shorts
* Linen outfits

#### Poor

* Gym wear
* Technical sportswear

---

### Wardrobe Notes

* One excellent everyday watch provides more value than several fashion watches.
* Prioritize timeless proportions over complications.
* Brown leather should generally be the first strap if buying only one.

---

### Avoid

* Fashion-brand watches
* Oversized cases
* Loud dial designs
* Gold-tone plating
* Excessive complications

---
