# 05 – Outfit Library

## Purpose

This document is the operational guide for daily dressing.

It combines garments from [04_Wardrobe.md](04_Wardrobe.md) into ready-to-wear outfits, organized by occasion. It is not a style inspiration document — every outfit below uses only garments currently owned.

Where a category cannot be fully served by the current wardrobe, that gap is stated explicitly rather than filled with an invented garment. Gaps referenced here are owned by [04_Wardrobe.md](04_Wardrobe.md#shopping-priorities); this document only notes their effect on outfit-building.

---

## How to Read This Document

Each outfit lists **Trousers, Shoes, Top, Layering, Accessories**.

Each outfit carries a status:

* **Confirmed** — pairing already worn and recorded as such in [04_Wardrobe.md](04_Wardrobe.md).
* **Recommended** — a logical combination of owned garments, consistent with fit, colour, and role, but not yet worn in practice.

Reasoning follows the Decision Hierarchy in [02_Decision_Framework.md](02_Decision_Framework.md): silhouette and proportions first, then fit, role, versatility, and colour.

---

## Design Principles Applied

These principles apply across every outfit below and are not repeated per entry:

* **Silhouette & proportions** — slim build with balanced proportions and narrow-average shoulders favours slim (not skinny) fits that skim the body. Layering (overshirt, tailoring) is used deliberately to add structure across the shoulder line rather than bulk at the waist.
* **Colour** — every combination sits inside the Deep Autumn palette from [01_Personal_Profile.md](01_Personal_Profile.md). Warm neutrals (black, cream, brown, navy, olive) form the base; icy or cool-toned pieces are absent from the wardrobe and therefore never appear here.
* **Masculinity & elegance** — achieved through fit precision and colour restraint rather than pattern, branding, or accessories. Where the wardrobe's fit is inconsistent (see ready-made shirts in 04), tailored pieces are preferred as the anchor.
