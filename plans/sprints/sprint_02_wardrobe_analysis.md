# Sprint 2 — Wardrobe Analysis & Shopping Priorities

**Status:** Complete

## Objective

Use the completed wardrobe inventory together with the Decision Framework and Garment Specifications to evaluate the wardrobe as a complete system.

The goal is to identify opportunities for improvement, not to recommend products.

## Required Analysis

### Strengths

Identify areas where the wardrobe already performs well.

Examples:
- Good office wardrobe
- Strong knitwear
- Versatile footwear

### Weaknesses

Identify structural weaknesses.

Examples:
- Insufficient casual trousers
- Lack of layering
- Duplicate colours

### Category Coverage

For every garment category, determine status:
- Complete
- Adequate
- Underdeveloped
- Missing

### Shopping Priorities

Create priority tiers (Tier 1, Tier 2, Tier 3). Each recommendation should explain:
- Why it matters
- Opportunity cost
- Expected versatility gain

Include only garment categories, not specific products.

### Duplication

Identify garments performing identical roles.

### Bottlenecks

Identify wardrobe limitations preventing outfit creation.

## Success Criteria

The resulting analysis should directly answer:

- What should I buy next?
- What should I stop buying?
- Which categories are already complete?
