Runtime reference distilled from [`docs/05_Outfit_Library.md`](../docs/05_Outfit_Library.md). Do not edit directly — update the source and re-derive. Every outfit uses only owned garments; stated gaps are owned by [04_Wardrobe.md](04_Wardrobe.md#shopping-priorities).

**Status:** Confirmed = worn and recorded. Recommended = untested but logical combination of owned garments.
**Reasoning order** per [02_Decision_Framework.md](02_Decision_Framework.md): silhouette/proportions > fit > role > versatility > colour.

## Design Principles (apply to all outfits below)
- Slim, balanced, narrow-average shoulders → slim (not skinny) fits; layering adds shoulder-line structure, not waist bulk.
- All combinations stay inside the Deep Autumn palette; no icy/cool-toned pieces owned.
- Masculinity/elegance comes from fit precision and colour restraint; tailored pieces anchor an outfit over ready-made where fit is inconsistent.

## Wardrobe Gaps Affecting This Library
- No knitwear — Business Casual relies on dressed-down formal shirts instead of a true polo.
- Single everyday shoe (Salvador) — nearly every outfit routes through it; its toe-box fit issue affects the whole library.
- No formal-occasion watch — Wedding (Western) goes bare-wrist.
- No dedicated festival wear — Festivals borrow from Wedding and Weekend.
