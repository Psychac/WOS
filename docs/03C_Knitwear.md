# 03C – Knitwear

## Purpose

This document defines the characteristics of well-designed knit tops and explains how to evaluate them.

It is a permanent reference manual and does not contain shopping priorities or brand recommendations.

---

# Part I — Garment Fundamentals

This section defines concepts that apply across all garment categories.

## 1. Formality Scale

Every garment exists somewhere on a spectrum rather than in a fixed category.

- Formal
- Business
- Business Casual
- Smart Casual
- Casual
- Relaxed Casual

A garment should be evaluated within its intended level of formality rather than against garments serving different purposes.

---

## 2. Fit

Fit describes how a garment follows the body's dimensions.

A good fit should:

- Allow unrestricted movement
- Follow the body's shape without excessive tightness
- Avoid unnecessary excess fabric
- Support the intended silhouette

Fit is independent of style.

A relaxed-fit garment can fit correctly.

A slim-fit garment can fit poorly.

---

## 3. Silhouette

Silhouette is the visual outline created by the body and clothing together.

Silhouette is more important than numerical size.

The desired silhouette should support the wearer's proportions and intended style identity.

---

## 4. Structure

Structure is the ability of a garment to create or maintain shape.

Sources of structure include:

- Fabric weight
- Interfacing
- Construction
- Collar design
- Shoulder design

Greater structure generally increases visual authority and masculinity, while excessive structure can reduce comfort and appear overly formal.

---

## 5. Drape

Drape describes how fabric hangs on the body.

Good drape should follow the intended silhouette without clinging or collapsing.

---

## 6. Visual Weight

Visual weight is the perceived prominence of a garment.

It is influenced by:

- Colour value
- Texture
- Fabric weight
- Pattern
- Volume

Visual weight should be distributed intentionally to create balanced proportions.

---

## 7. Texture

Texture creates visual interest without relying on loud patterns.

Examples include:

- Oxford cloth
- Twill
- Linen
- Corduroy
- Brushed cotton

Texture is generally preferred over bold prints for long-term versatility.

---

## 8. Construction Quality

Construction determines durability, shape retention and finishing quality.

Indicators include:

- Stitching consistency
- Pattern alignment
- Reinforcement
- Hardware quality
- Seam finishing

Construction should be evaluated independently from branding.

---

## 9. Wardrobe Role

Every garment should perform one primary function within the wardrobe.

Examples:

- Office foundation
- Everyday casual
- Evening smart casual
- Travel
- Seasonal layering

Garments without a clear role should generally be avoided.

---

## 10. Versatility

Versatility is the ability of a garment to integrate naturally into multiple outfits and occasions.

It should be evaluated by practical use rather than theoretical combinations.

---

# Part IV – Knit Tops

## Philosophy

Knit tops provide texture, softness and visual depth while occupying the space between shirts and outerwear.

For this wardrobe, knit tops should prioritize refined simplicity, tactile fabrics and versatility over loud branding or trend-driven styling.

---

## Universal Principles

- Prioritize texture over graphics.
- Prioritize natural fibres over synthetics.
- Fit should skim the body rather than cling.
- Heavy logos and graphics reduce versatility.
- Knitwear should complement, not compete with, the rest of the outfit.

---

## Foundation vs Accent

### Foundation Knit Tops

Designed for frequent wear and maximum versatility.

Examples:

- Polo Shirts
- Knit Polos
- Crewneck Sweaters

### Accent Knit Tops

Used to introduce seasonal texture or layering.

Examples:

- Quarter-Zips
- Cardigans

## Common Pitfalls

These issues appear across multiple knit types:

* **Heavy logos and bold graphics** — Reduces versatility and violates the principle that knitwear should complement, not compete
* **Loud synthetic finishes** — Conflicts with the priority of natural fibres and tactile quality
* **Bold or high-contrast stripes** — Creates visual competition with the outfit
* **Clingy or oversized fits** — Should skim the body, not emphasize shape or create shapelessness

## 4.1 Polo Shirt

### Importance Tier
Tier S — Foundation

### Quick Spec

| Category         | Ideal                                                            |
| ---------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Fit              | Relaxed Slim                                                     |
| Collar & Placket | Structured Collar, 2-Button Placket                              |
| Fabric           | Piqué Cotton or Textured Cotton                                  |
| Construction     | Medium Structure                                                 |
| Colours          | Olive, Dark Olive, Forest Green, Tobacco, Cream, Ecru, Warm Navy |
| Pattern          | Solid                                                            |

---

### Role

A versatile smart-casual foundation that bridges the gap between T-shirts and button-down shirts.

Provides a more refined appearance than a T-shirt while remaining more relaxed than a shirt.

---

### When I Need It

* Smart casual office
* Casual Friday
* Weekends
* Date nights
* Dinner
* Travel
* Summer

---

### Design Specification

#### Fit

##### Ideal

* Relaxed Slim
* Clean shoulders
* Comfortable chest
* Gentle waist suppression
* Sleeve ends around mid-bicep

##### Acceptable

* Tailored

##### Avoid

* Extra Slim
* Oversized
* Long, loose sleeves

##### Why

* Creates a masculine silhouette.
* Prevents the polo from looking either sloppy or restrictive.
* Layers comfortably under lightweight jackets and overshirts.

---

#### Collar & Placket

##### Ideal

* Structured collar that holds its shape
* Medium collar size
* 2-button placket
* Clean placket construction

##### Acceptable

* 3-button placket

##### Avoid

* Soft collapsing collars
* Tiny collars
* Oversized collars
* Deep plackets
* Contrast collars

##### Why

* The collar defines the appearance of a polo.
* A structured collar maintains a refined look throughout the day.
* Two-button plackets provide the cleanest proportions.

---

#### Fabric

##### Ideal

* 100% Cotton Piqué
* Textured Cotton Knit

##### Acceptable

* Cotton Blend (minimal synthetic content)

##### Avoid

* Polyester-rich blends
* Thin jersey fabric (T-shirt style)
* High-shine materials

##### Why

* Piqué provides breathability and subtle texture.
* Structured fabrics maintain shape better over time.
* Texture adds interest without relying on graphics.

---

#### Construction

##### Ideal

* Medium structure
* Ribbed sleeve cuffs
* Clean hem
* Minimal branding
* Quality stitching

##### Acceptable

* Side vents

##### Avoid

* Large embroidered logos
* Contrast trims
* Decorative piping
* Heavy branding

##### Why

* Keeps the polo timeless and versatile.
* Allows the fit and fabric to remain the focus.

---

#### Colours

##### Ideal

* Olive
* Dark Olive
* Forest Green
* Tobacco
* Cream
* Ecru
* Warm Navy

##### Acceptable

* Rust
* Terracotta
* Chocolate Brown

##### Avoid

* Bright Blue
* Neon
* Cool Grey
* Optical White
* Large colour blocking

##### Why

* Earthy tones strengthen a Deep Autumn wardrobe.
* Neutral colours maximize outfit compatibility.

---

#### Pattern

##### Ideal

* Solid

##### Acceptable

* Fine texture
* Melange knit

##### Avoid

* High-contrast colour blocking

##### Why

* The polo should be a versatile foundation piece.
* Texture should provide visual interest instead of loud patterns.

---

### Outfit Compatibility

#### Excellent

* Chinos
* Dark Indigo Jeans
* Linen Trousers
* Minimal Leather Sneakers
* Brown Loafers
* Harrington Jackets
* Overshirts

##### Good

* Shorts
* Crewneck Sweaters (layered)

##### Poor

* Business Suits
* Black Tie
* Formal Tailoring

---

### Wardrobe Notes

* One of the highest-value garments in the wardrobe.
* Prioritize collar quality over brand.
* Earthy colours should dominate this category.
* Piqué should be the default fabric unless there is a deliberate reason to choose another knit.

---

### Avoid

* Large chest logos
* Skinny fit
* Polyester-rich fabric
* Soft collapsing collar
* Graphic prints
* Colour-blocked designs
* Trend-driven details

---

* Better suited to your desired aesthetic.

* Ribbed cuffs and hem
* Minimal branding
* Clean finishing

##### Acceptable

##### Avoid

* Decorative trims
* Heavy branding

##### Why

* Warm Navy

#### Pattern
