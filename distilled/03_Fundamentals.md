# 03 – Garment Fundamentals (Runtime)

Runtime reference distilled from Part I of [`docs/03A_Shirts.md`](../docs/03A_Shirts.md) (identical across `03A`–`03G`). Do not edit directly — update the source and re-derive. Applies to every garment category in `distilled/03A`–`03G`.

- **Formality Scale** — Formal > Business > Business Casual > Smart Casual > Casual > Relaxed Casual. Evaluate a garment against its own formality level, not garments from another level.
- **Fit** — follows body shape without excessive tightness or excess fabric; allows unrestricted movement; supports intended silhouette. Independent of style (a relaxed fit can fit correctly; a slim fit can fit poorly).
- **Silhouette** — the visual outline of body + clothing; more important than numerical size; must support the wearer's proportions and style identity.
- **Structure** — ability to create/maintain shape (fabric weight, interfacing, construction, collar/shoulder design). More structure = more visual authority; too much reduces comfort and reads overly formal.
- **Drape** — how fabric hangs; should follow the silhouette without clinging or collapsing.
- **Visual Weight** — perceived prominence (colour value, texture, fabric weight, pattern, volume); distribute intentionally for balanced proportions.
- **Texture** — visual interest without loud pattern (oxford, twill, linen, corduroy, brushed cotton); generally preferred over bold prints for versatility.
- **Construction Quality** — stitching consistency, pattern alignment, reinforcement, hardware, seam finishing; evaluate independent of brand.
- **Wardrobe Role** — one clear primary function (office foundation, everyday casual, evening smart casual, travel, seasonal layering); avoid garments without a clear role.
- **Versatility** — ability to integrate into multiple outfits/occasions in practice, not theory.
