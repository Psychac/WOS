## Decision Hierarchy (evaluate in order)

1. **Silhouette** — improves/maintains intended masculine silhouette; overrides colour, fabric, price, brand.
2. **Proportions** — length, rise, leg opening, collar size correct for the body; prerequisite to silhouette.
3. **Fit** — comfortable, no excess tension/volume; never compensates for poor silhouette.
4. **Wardrobe Role** — one clear primary role; no role duplication unless replacing an inferior item.
5. **Versatility** — measured by real outfit creation, not theoretical combinations.
6. **Colour Harmony** — priority: Warm > Muted > Earthy > Deep Autumn compatible.
7. **Fabric & Texture** — structure, durability, comfort, seasonal performance, visual richness; texture before pattern.
8. **Construction Quality** — stitching, collar construction, fabric stability, buttons, hardware, finishing.
9. **Price-to-Value** — higher price acceptable only with meaningful quality/longevity/versatility gain.
10. **Brand** — lowest priority; never compensates for weaknesses elsewhere; tiebreaker only.

## Purchase Algorithm (answer in order; any "no" per rule below → Reject/Delay)

1. Objectively well-designed? No → Reject.
2. Suits body type? No → Reject.
3. Suits colour palette? No → Reject unless compelling reason.
4. Fills existing gap? No → Proceed only if replacing an inferior item.
5. Significantly increases outfit possibilities? No → Reject.
6. Highest-return purchase in current budget? No → Delay.
7. Still wanted without discount/brand recognition? No → Reject.

## Opportunity Cost Principle

Compare against the highest-impact competing purchase, not "item vs. nothing."

## Wardrobe Role Principle

Roles: Professional, Casual, Smart Casual, Evening, Travel, Layering, Summer, Winter, Gym. One role per garment.

## Rejection Rules (reject immediately on any match)

Poor silhouette · poor shoulder fit · skinny fit · extreme oversized fit · loud branding · excessive graphics · distressing · artificial fading · trend-driven design · poor colour harmony · requires multiple additional purchases before wearable.

## Replacement Rules (replace instead of expanding when)

No longer fits · construction deteriorated · colour no longer aligns · new item performs same role significantly better · replacement meaningfully increases versatility.

## Trade-off Rules (conflict resolution order)

Silhouette > Fit > Wardrobe Role > Versatility > Colour > Fabric > Construction > Price > Brand.

## Decision Outcomes

- **Buy** — strongly satisfies framework, high priority, excellent budget use.
- **Consider** — good option, trade-offs exist, evaluate against alternatives.
- **Skip** — fails a high-priority criterion, or opportunity cost too high.

## Long-Term Philosophy

Each purchase should increase cohesion, increase outfit quality, reduce decision fatigue, strengthen intended image. Objective: a wardrobe that performs its function with minimal compromise, not more clothing.

## Permanent Footwear Constraints

**Foot profile:** wide forefoot/metatarsal, above-average toe splay, medium-to-high instep, big toe ≈ second toe length.

**Fit issues:** toe box cramped before length insufficient; top-of-foot pressure; standard widths often force unnecessary up-sizing.

**Decision priority:** Last shape > Forefoot width > Instep volume > Overall fit > Size. Never size up before confirming last shape.

**Avoid:** narrow Italian lasts, aggressive almond/pointed toes, low-volume shoes, narrow toe boxes, models known to run narrow.

**Prefer:** anatomically generous toe boxes, medium/wide lasts, moderate toe taper, medium-to-high instep volume.

## Footwear Decision Framework

**Evaluation priority:** Foot compatibility > Last & Shape > Construction > Upper Material > Comfort > Sole > Colour > Brand.

**Foot compatibility rules:** forefoot width before length; instep volume before aesthetics; never size up before last check; reject narrow/aggressively tapered toe boxes; avoid narrow-running lasts; prefer medium/wide lasts with generous forefoot volume.

**Every footwear recommendation must state:** width compatibility, last characteristics, narrow/standard/wide tendency, sizing advice (after width), confidence of fit for this foot profile.
