### Blazer
Tier: A — Core
Role: Foundation tailored garment; bridges formal and smart-casual dressing with structure, refinement, versatility.
Ideal: Silhouette: Single-breasted, two-button, moderate lapels, jacket covers seat, balanced button stance · Fit: Tailored, clean chest, suppressed waist, comfortable sleeve mobility · Shoulders: Lightly structured, natural line, clean sleeve head · Fabric: 100% Wool, Tropical Wool, Fresco Wool · Construction: Half-canvas, double vents, functional inner pockets, horn-style buttons, minimal branding · Colours: Warm Navy, Chocolate Brown, Tobacco · Pattern: Solid, Birdseye, Hopsack, fine texture
Acceptable: Silhouette: Three-roll-two front · Shoulders: Soft Neapolitan · Fabric: Wool blend (high wool content) · Fit: Relaxed tailoring · Construction: Well-made fused construction · Colours: Charcoal Brown, Olive (casual blazer) · Pattern: Prince of Wales (low contrast)
Avoid: Very short jackets, long fashion cuts, double-breasted (first blazer); heavy padding, dropped shoulders, rope shoulders; polyester-dominant blends, high-shine fabrics, heavy synthetic textures; skinny fit, boxy fit, oversized silhouettes; single vent; bright blue, jet black, loud colours; bold windowpane, loud checks, high-contrast patterns
Colours: Warm Navy, Chocolate Brown, Tobacco
Evaluation order: Fit(30%) > Silhouette(20%) > Fabric(20%) > Shoulders(15%) > Construction(10%) > Colour & Pattern(5%)
Wardrobe role: Foundation — highest-ROI tailored garment, warm navy is the default first colour.

### Suit
Tier: A — Core
Role: Primary formal garment; business, weddings, ceremonies, formal occasions requiring complete tailoring.
Ideal: Silhouette: Single-breasted, two-button front, moderate lapels, jacket covers seat · Shoulders: Lightly structured, clean line · Fabric: 100% Wool, Tropical Wool · Fit: Tailored, comfortable chest, suppressed waist, clean trouser line · Construction: Half-canvas, double vents, flat-front trousers, horn-style buttons · Colours: Warm Navy, Charcoal · Pattern: Solid
Acceptable: Silhouette: Three-roll-two · Shoulders: Natural shoulder · Fabric: Wool blend (high wool content) · Construction: Quality fused construction · Colours: Medium Grey · Pattern: Birdseye, fine pinstripe
Avoid: Skinny fashion suits, oversized cuts, double-breasted (first suit); heavy padding, dropped shoulders; polyester-rich fabrics, high-shine finishes; skinny fit, boxy fit; single vent; black (first suit), bright blue, loud colours; bold checks, loud windowpane
Colours: Warm Navy, Charcoal
Evaluation order: Fit(30%) > Silhouette(20%) > Fabric(20%) > Shoulders(15%) > Construction(10%) > Colour(5%)
Wardrobe role: Foundation — own fewer, better suits; fit and alterations matter more than brand; warm navy first.

### Sport Coat
Tier: A — Core
Role: Smart-casual tailored jacket introducing texture and personality while remaining refined.
Ideal: Silhouette: Single-breasted, two-button, slightly softer structure · Fabric: Wool, Tweed, Hopsack, Linen-Wool Blend · Colours: Olive, Tobacco, Brown, Warm Navy · Pattern: Herringbone, Hopsack, subtle check
Acceptable: Fabric: Cotton twill · Colours: Forest Green, Rust · Pattern: Prince of Wales (low contrast)
Avoid: Oversized tailoring, very short jackets; polyester-rich fabrics, high-shine materials; loud contrasts; bold plaid, high-contrast windowpane; shiny fabrics, skinny tailoring, heavy branding
Colours: Olive, Tobacco, Brown, Warm Navy
Evaluation order: Fit(30%) > Silhouette(25%) > Fabric(20%) > Pattern(15%) > Colour(10%)
Wardrobe role: Foundation/Accent — texture is the defining feature; own one versatile piece before bolder patterns.

### Waistcoat
Tier: C — Specialist
Role: Optional tailoring piece for extra refinement/warmth, primarily as part of a three-piece suit.
Ideal: Silhouette: Five/six-button single-breasted, covers waistband completely, clean V-opening · Fit: Close to body, comfortable movement · Fabric: Matching suit cloth, Wool · Colours: Match the suit · Pattern: Solid
Acceptable: Colours: Tonal neutral (for separates)
Avoid: Fashion waistcoats, very short lengths; tight pulling, excess fabric; shiny satin (except formal eveningwear); contrasting novelty colours; novelty backs, loud patterns, skinny fits, decorative chains
Colours: Match the suit
Evaluation order: Fit(30%) > Silhouette(25%) > Fabric(20%) > Construction(15%) > Colour(10%)
Wardrobe role: Specialist — buy only after core tailoring wardrobe is complete; treat as enhancement not necessity.

### Tuxedo
Tier: C — Specialist
Role: Highest level of formal tailoring, reserved exclusively for black-tie events.
Ideal: Silhouette: Single-breasted, one-button closure, proper evening proportions · Lapels: Shawl or Peak · Fabric: 100% Wool · Construction: Half-canvas · Colours: Midnight Navy or Black · Pattern: Solid
Acceptable: Fabric: Wool-Mohair blend
Avoid: Fashion tuxedos, skinny tailoring; notch lapels; shiny synthetic fabrics; white jackets (unless required), fashion colours; decorative trims, loud styling
Colours: Midnight Navy, Black
Evaluation order: Fit(30%) > Silhouette(25%) > Fabric(20%) > Lapels(15%) > Construction(10%)
Wardrobe role: Specialist — rent before buying unless black-tie events are frequent; fit matters more than luxury branding.
